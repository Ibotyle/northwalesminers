Billins Lead Mine

Location
Pentre Halkyn, Flintshire SJ19607200
Period of Operation
Early 19th Century to 1872
Type
Lead Mine

History

The Billins Mine worked the Pant Vein until 1872, when the sett was taken over by the Prince Patrick Mining Company.

Lead mining was once a major industry on Halkyn Mountain, with numerous mines extracting lead ore from the rich veins in the limestone bedrock.

Current Remains

Information from Clwyd-Powys Archaeological Trust (CPAT):

Evidence of workings remain as grassed-over shafts and spoil heaps near the summit of Halkyn Mountain. The old shafts cover the area immediately to the south of the Pentre Halkyn to Babell Road.

The cottages at SJ19657210 were probably mine-related, providing housing for workers at the mine.
